# Future & Market

Why Physical AI is widely considered the next major growth frontier in computing — with the numbers, the milestones, and the honest caveats.

## Why it's considered futuristic and high-growth
- **The labor-market thesis:** proponents (NVIDIA's Jensen Huang among the loudest) argue that intelligent machines could address a multi-trillion-dollar global labor market — a far larger TAM than software alone.
- **The "ChatGPT moment" framing:** at CES 2025 (Jan 6, 2025), Huang argued that "the ChatGPT moment for general robotics is just around the corner" — i.e., that foundation models + simulation + edge compute have reached the point where general-purpose robots become practical.
- **The enabling stack matured at once:** capable VLA policies ([OpenVLA, π0, GR00T](../vla-and-world-models/README.md)), world models for synthetic data ([Cosmos](../vla-and-world-models/README.md)), and edge silicon powerful enough to run them ([Jetson Thor, Hailo-10H](../companies/README.md)) all arrived together in 2024–2026.

## Milestones & facts (a short history)
- **2023** — RT-2 (Google DeepMind) demonstrates the VLA paradigm: a single model mapping vision+language to robot actions.
- **2024** — OpenVLA (7B, open) and π0 push open and SOTA policies; NVIDIA Cosmos world-model platform announced.
- **Jan 2025** — Huang's CES keynote reframes robotics as NVIDIA's next frontier; Blaize becomes the first AI-chip startup to go public via SPAC.
- **Mid-2025** — Hailo-10H GA brings generative AI to edge accelerators; SiMa.ai, EnCharge, and Axelera ship/announce next-gen parts.
- **Late 2025–2026** — Mythic raises $125M for analog AI; Qualcomm enters the data center; Boston Dynamics' electric Atlas runs Jetson Thor; China targets 10,000+ humanoids manufactured in 2025.
- **2026** — Raspberry Pi AI HAT+ 2 (Hailo-10H) puts on-device GenAI on a $130 add-on; DeepX nears mass production and an IPO.

Industry scale signal: **2.2M+ developers across 7,000 companies** build on NVIDIA's robotics/Jetson stack.

## Honest caveats
- **The market numbers are not reliable to one significant figure** — they disagree by an order of magnitude. Use them for direction, not precision, and always cite scope + source + year.
- **Humanoid timelines are contested.** Production claims (e.g., Tesla Optimus Gen 3) are often vendor-stated; independent verification lags.
- **Hardware claims are vendor-sourced** unless a third party has tested them (see the [hardware comparison](../hardware-comparison/README.md) caveats).
